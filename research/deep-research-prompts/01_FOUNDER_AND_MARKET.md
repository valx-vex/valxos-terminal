# Master Prompt: Founder And Market Research

You are doing deep product and market research for `VALXOS Terminal`.

Product concept:
- A provider-agnostic AI coding terminal and control center
- Supports Claude, OpenAI/Codex, Gemini, and Ollama from one interface
- Wants Claude Code level usefulness and extension compatibility
- Longer-term UX target is closer to a Warp-class control center than a plain CLI
- Intended to be broadly accessible, ideally free/open where possible

Your task:
- Research the product landscape around AI coding terminals, agentic CLIs, terminal copilots, command centers, and developer AI workbenches
- Identify where users are still underserved
- Identify what would make VALXOS clearly different and worth adopting

Research questions:
- What products are closest today in capability or vision?
- How do Claude Code, OpenCode, Warp, Cursor terminal features, Gemini CLI style tools, and local-model shells compare?
- What are the strongest user pains around model fragmentation, context fragmentation, plugin fragmentation, and workflow fragmentation?
- What features are table stakes versus differentiators?
- What business/open-source models seem sustainable for a public-facing free product?
- Which user segments should VALXOS target first?

Method:
- Prioritize official product docs, repos, changelogs, pricing pages, launch posts, and high-signal reviews
- Include a few strong user-voice sources like GitHub issues, Hacker News, Reddit, or community threads
- Prefer recent information

Deliverables:
- Executive summary
- Market map with 8-15 competitors or adjacent products
- Feature comparison matrix
- Clear product positioning statement for VALXOS
- Top 10 opportunities
- Top 10 risks
- Recommended MVP wedge
- Recommended messaging for builders, power users, and open-source users

Output format:
- Markdown
- Use short sections
- Include a source list with links
- Mark each major claim as either `Validated` or `Inference`
